About

My Story

I’m a UX Researcher with a background in English literature. I started my career as a storyteller. Working in PR for higher education, I researched to understand what set my clients’ stories apart from the competition. I immersed myself in the media landscape to know exactly what outlet and journalist would be receptive to the pitch. Then, I crafted a story pitch that would capture their attention. As exciting as it is to get someone on NPR, the results of a good PR campaign can be somewhat intangible. So I’m bringing my insatiable curiosity to the field of user experience design where I (and my users) can enjoy the tangible results of my work.

When building a house, you wouldn’t skip the foundation. You certainly wouldn’t skip the frame — what would hold up the roof? Just like a sturdy house needs its foundation and frame, a usable design must be built upon user research. It’s my goal as a User Experience Researcher to build delightful experiences for users.

I’ve been that frustrated user who wondered why an interface changed and why nobody asked me what I wanted. Now, I get to be the person who asks users what their needs and goals are. And it is so very rewarding when I can validate a design with target users during a usability study.

I was born in Bad Ischl, Austria and moved to Pennsylvania when I was 6 years old. Growing up feeling at home on two different continents has given me a perspective on our world that’s made me curious to always ask why and to interrogate the stories we tell about ourselves and our world. I channel that inquisitiveness and desire to make things better into my research and design work.

When I’m not busy saving the world from poor design, I’m either reading or out in nature (possibly also reading). I love hiking, kayaking, and exploring our beautiful planet. If you happen to meet me on a video call, you’re sure to also meet one or both of my cats, Francis and Walter (our dog is camera shy).

I’m always looking for opportunities to engage in creative problem solving. If you’ve got a job or freelance opportunity or would like to collaborate on a project, send me a message.
